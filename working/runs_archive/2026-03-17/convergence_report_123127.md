## Abstract

We test whether semantic commitment survives multi-turn conversational cascade under four anchor-input regimes (all-anchor, half-anchor, two-anchor, one-anchor) across two turn-depth conditions (standard=8, double=16). Enforcement = commitment kernel extraction + re-injection if lost. Metric = total token count per turn (word-count approximation, matching original hand-logged methodology from founding test). The Conservation Law predicts: C(T(S)) < C(S) without enforcement.

## Methodology

- **Corpus:** 20 canonical signals across 20 commitment categories  
- **Model:** GPT-4o-mini (OpenAI) for both AI and human-simulation turns  
- **Anchor turns:** Original signal repeated as input (replicates hand-logged methodology)  
- **Cascade turns:** Previous AI output fed directly as next input  
- **Enforcement:** Regex extraction of hard modals (must/shall/cannot/required/never/always); kernel re-injected if absent  
- **Token metric:** Word-count (matches original hand-logged methodology)
